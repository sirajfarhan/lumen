# Question Sharpener Instruments

Use this reference when the discovery target is broad enough that one question may overfit the first visible angle.

Discovery works by reducing hiddenness. Choose the instrument by asking how the thing is hidden. A sharp question is powerful when the uncertainty can be cut into meaningful alternatives. Other cases need observation or incident work. Some need frame pressure. Some need relation mapping, causal tests, or evidence synthesis.

The resilient path also asks how close the inquiry is to reality. Do not treat every answer as equal evidence. A stated account can be useful, but it may only show what the person can say safely or remember cleanly. A stronger inquiry moves closer when the consequence demands it.

Efficiency in discovery means the least sufficient contact with the hidden thing. A single sharpened question is efficient when the target is stable and reachable evidence can answer it. A longer inquiry path earns its cost only when the first answer would stay performative or too far from consequence.

Attention leak is a wrong-contact problem. The inquiry starts contacting an easier side frame instead of the hidden thing the user asked to uncover. That can feel prudent while lowering relevance. Keep the accepted question under discussion visible. Add adjacent concerns only when they change the instrument, contact rung, or decisive test.

## Hiddenness To Instrument

When description is hidden, open the terrain. Ask for the lived sequence and the normal case. The aim is to see what actually happens before theory explains it.

When practice is hidden, enter an incident. Ask for a recent case and walk through the cues. Recover the decision and expectation. Then ask what surprised them, what they rejected, and which condition changed the action. The aim is to catch knowledge while it is being used.

When expertise is hidden, probe judgment. Ask what an expert notices early and what would worry them before others see a problem. The answer should expose discrimination, not polish an explanation.

When a boundary is hidden, use contrast. Compare a nearby case, a near miss, and a falsifying case. The aim is to learn where the current frame stops working.

When a frame is hidden, hold rival standpoints. Ask what problem this is under the starting frame. Then change the boundary or stakeholder and ask what problem appears now.

When system behavior is hidden, map relations. Name the actors and the flows that connect them. Then ask what property appears only because the parts interact.

When causality is hidden, separate association from mechanism. A descriptive pattern can show what moves together. A causal inquiry asks what would change if one condition changed.

When the state of knowledge is hidden, map the evidence record. Ask where support is dense and where it is thin. Ask which claims repeat from one origin and what missing evidence would change the decision.

## Reality Contact

A stated account is enough when the question is low risk and the person can answer from clear memory. It becomes weak when social pressure may shape the answer. It also becomes weak when fear or self-protection controls what can be said.

A concrete incident is stronger when the target lives in sequence. Ask for a recent moment and recover what happened before explanation takes over.

Observation is stronger when work-as-described may differ from work-as-done. Watch the task or inspect the artifact where the work leaves traces. Use follow-up questions after the action has made the hidden work visible.

Logs and records are stronger when memory may smooth frequency or timing. Treat them as another partial view. A log can show a pattern without explaining the human reason behind it.

Expert-research is stronger when the local story may be missing domain vocabulary or outside evidence. Use it before finalizing the premise when the topic is regulated or technical. Use it when the facts are current. Use it when the consequence is high.

A small reversible test is stronger when the inquiry needs causal contact. Use it when the best answer depends on what changes the outcome rather than what people believe caused it.

## Instrument Families

Discriminating questions divide the answer space. They work when the main uncertainty already has live alternatives.

Descriptive questions reveal terrain. They work when the user does not yet know the lived shape of the thing.

Incident probes reveal tacit knowledge. They work when general explanation becomes too smooth.

Contrasts and counterexamples reveal boundaries. They work when a plausible frame has not met a nearby exception.

Frames and reframes reveal standpoint. They work when the first problem definition may be the cover.

Maps and models reveal relations. They work when the thing disappears if parts are studied alone.

Experiments and counterfactuals reveal mechanism. They work when the user needs to know what makes a difference.

Evidence maps reveal the shape of what is known. They work when the hidden thing is the distribution of evidence.

Synthesis reveals what now holds together. It works after partial views have made the object visible from more than one side.

## Sharpness Dimensions

Partition sharpness asks whether the question separates possibilities that matter.

Granularity sharpness asks whether the question is aimed at the right unit.

Perspective sharpness asks whether the question makes its standpoint visible.

Causal sharpness asks whether the question knows which causal level it is using.

Evidential sharpness asks whether the available evidence can answer the question.

Corrective sharpness asks whether the question leaves room for disconfirmation and surprise.

Reality sharpness asks whether the question will contact the right kind of evidence. A question aimed at a risky public account is dull even when the wording is elegant. A question becomes sharper when it leads to a lived moment. It can also become sharper through a trace, contrast, or test because the answer has less room to perform.

## Discovery Set

A full-view inquiry needs enough instruments to avoid mistaking one face for the whole. Begin wide when the starting frame feels too tight. Enter a concrete case when abstraction is smoothing away the work. Add a serious counterexample when the favored answer arrives too easily. Map relations when the object changes through interaction. Synthesize when the user needs to see both the object and the path that revealed it.

The normal rhythm is:

```text
widen -> focus -> probe -> test -> reframe -> synthesize
```

Do not force the full rhythm on a simple target. Use only the steps needed to reveal the target and expose the strongest distortion risk.

## Runtime Shape

Keep target and cover diagnosis serial because a wrong target makes every later branch efficient in the wrong direction. Keep final wording after instrument choice because warmer wording can accidentally remove the contact the instrument was meant to create.

Parallelize only after the target, hiddenness type, and contact rung are stable. Independent branches may inspect artifacts or outside evidence. Each branch should return one bounded observation that changes the inquiry or names the remaining distortion risk. Reunify those observations before final wording or handoff.

Stop when the next branch would only add another angle without changing the instrument choice, the reality-contact rung, or the next decisive test.

## Resilience Patterns

When the answer may be socially safe, begin with permission and a concrete moment. The repair is to ask what happened before the official reason appeared.

When the issue may involve blame, begin with system language. The repair is to ask where the normal path stopped fitting the work.

When the respondent may not understand the question as intended, ask what the wording means to them or pilot the question before trusting it.

When the local explanation is plausible but untested, add a rival case. The repair is to compare a case where the explanation should apply with one where it should not.

When the agent has many possible explanations, do not feed them to the respondent as a menu. Ask for the lived moment first. Use the explanation set as a listening lens after the answer begins to show its shape.

When the agent has many possible moral or worldview interpretations, keep them out of the visible question unless the user asked to examine that interpretation. Treat them as possible distortion risks, not as the target. If none changes the next evidence contact, cut them.

When the question depends on domain knowledge, use expert-research before wording the final probe. The repair is to learn the right boundary and vocabulary before asking people to answer inside it.

When a causal answer would change action, add a small test. The repair is to ask what would have to change and how the user would know it changed the outcome.

## Return Evidence

A strong discovery output shows what was observed and what was inferred. It also shows the active frame and the rejected alternative. It names thin evidence, the assumed boundary, and the test that would reduce the remaining uncertainty.

When returning important questions, do not only list them. Name the instrument family and the reveal. Then name the hidden cost and the check.

When writer shapes the final wording, preserve the instrument. A warmer line that loses the incident, contrast, or test has become softer but less true. Rewrite until the line still reaches the chosen evidence.

For important inquiry paths, cold review belongs outside the generator. Ask an independent fresh agent to read only the final question or path and say what it thinks the inquiry is trying to reveal, what evidence contact it asks for, and what distortion it guards against. If that reader reconstructs a different target or a softer contact rung, repair the inquiry path before returning it as ready.
